SECTION 1 - FLOOR 3 IDENTITY
================================================================================

One-line identity:
FLOOR 3 = Structured Intelligence Factory

Meaning:
- Floor 2 gives clean, verified, replay-friendly data
- Floor 3 converts that data into rich structured analytical intelligence
- Floor 4 interprets that intelligence into expert opinion
- Floor 5 judges and decides

This floor exists because raw data alone is not usable.
But raw calculations alone are also not enough.
So Floor 3 must sit in the middle and generate structured intelligence that is:
- rich
- reusable
- debuggable
- domain-preserving
- non-opinionated

Floor 3 is not allowed to say:
- BUY
- SELL
- Strong Bullish Trade
- Captain should act now
- conviction = 85
- confidence = 70

That would break architecture.

SECTION 3 - ARCHITECTURE LAW OF FLOORS 3, 4, 5
================================================================================

This is one of the most important architectural laws in the entire system.
It must remain visible and explicit:

QUALITY   = FLOOR 3
CONFIDENCE = FLOOR 4
CONVICTION = FLOOR 5

Meaning:
- Floor 3 measures and structures quality of analytical conditions
- Floor 4 interprets those structured conditions and expresses expert confidence
- Floor 5 judges everything together and creates final conviction

Why this separation matters:
If Floor 3 starts generating confidence or conviction,
then Heads become weak and Captain becomes pointless.
If Floor 4 starts generating conviction,
then Captain becomes a messenger instead of a judge.
If Floor 5 starts recomputing quality,
then Floor 3 becomes redundant and Captain becomes bloated.

This separation must never be broken.

SECTION 8 - RAW OUTPUTS vs STRUCTURED STATES vs MAPS vs SCORES vs CANDIDATES
================================================================================

Every domain in Floor 3 must expose output in 5 clearly separated forms.
This is one of the most important implementation rules.

1. Raw Outputs
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
These are calculation results or direct derived values.
Examples:
- EMA values
- RSI values
- swing highs/lows
- OB coordinates
- FVG coordinates
- PCR values
- IV values

Why keep them:
- debugging
- transparency
- future model changes
- future logic re-use

2. Structured States
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
These are non-opinionated state labels.
Examples:
- trend_state = strong_up / weak_up / range / weak_down / strong_down
- smc_state = ob_active / fvg_active / sweep_confirmed / sweep_failed
- ict_state = discount_active / premium_active / displacement_valid / mss_active
- options_state = bullish_pressure / bearish_pressure / wall_near / pressure_neutral
- macro_state = calm / caution / event_risk

Why states matter:
- they reduce raw-number reading burden on Heads
- they do not force opinions
- they preserve structured intelligence

3. Maps
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Maps represent spatial market intelligence.
Examples:
- ob_map
- fvg_map
- liquidity_map
- level_map
- wall_map

Maps must exist in two forms:
- full internal maps
- active public maps

Why:
- full internal maps preserve depth and debugging
- active public maps reduce noise for Head consumption

4. Scores
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Scores are allowed in Floor 3,
but only as quality/state scores, not confidence or conviction.

Locked:
Tier 1 domains must have domain quality scores.
Examples:
- structure_strength_score
- smc_quality_score
- ict_delivery_score

Tier 2 / Tier 3 scores are optional.

Why scores are useful:
- they help Heads rank quality
- they support Captain indirectly through Head interpretation
- they do not replace confidence

5. Candidate Outputs
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Floor 3 may generate setup candidates.
This is locked.

But candidate output must remain non-opinionated.
Allowed examples:
- Potential Bullish FVG Retest Candidate
- Potential Sweep Reclaim Candidate
- Potential Continuation Candidate

Not allowed:
- Buy Now
- Strong Bullish Trade
- Approved Long

Why candidate outputs matter:
- they help Heads focus
- they reduce duplicate setup detection logic in Floor 4
- they preserve Head value because Heads still interpret and prioritize

SECTION 9 - COMMON MARKET STATE PROJECTION (INDEPENDENT BUILDER)
================================================================================

Locked:
Common Market State Projection must be defined as an independent builder.

Ownership
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Owner = Floor 3
Not Floor 4.
Not Captain.

Why ownership matters:
This is a secondary Floor 3 output.
If Floor 4 or Captain builds it, responsibility becomes blurred.

Purpose
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
To create a shared, lightweight, normalized market-state layer that multiple
consumers can use without flattening domain identity.

It does NOT replace domain packets.
It complements them.

Inputs
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
From Floor 3 domain outputs, projection builder consumes a selected subset of:
- price_state
- volatility_state
- session_state
- regime_state
- key_levels

Optional derived references from domain states can be included only if they remain
lightweight and common.

Outputs
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Common Market State Projection fields:
- price_state
- volatility_state
- session_state
- regime_state
- key_levels

This is intentionally narrow.
It should not become a giant everything-container.

Why this narrow scope is correct:
Because domain identity must remain preserved.
Common projection should remain small and useful, not replace the domain packets.

Builder role
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
common_market_state_projection.py should:
1. read selected outputs from all relevant domains
2. normalize them into shared common state
3. expose them as a lightweight projection packet

This projection will later help:
- heads in shared context reading
- floor summary creation
- dashboard lightweight state display
- debugging consistency

SECTION 18 - INTERNAL MAPS vs ACTIVE MAPS FLOW
================================================================================

Locked answer:
Use both.
- full internal maps
- active public maps

Why this is strong:
- full maps preserve depth, transparency, future upgrades, debugging
- active maps keep Head consumption focused and low-noise

Textual flow:

Clean Data
  -> Domain Detection Engines
  -> FULL INTERNAL MAPS created
      (all OBs / all FVGs / all liquidity points / all levels / all walls)
  -> Relevance / Activity Filtering
  -> ACTIVE PUBLIC MAPS created
      (only currently relevant / valid / head-consumable subset)
  -> Heads consume ACTIVE MAPS primarily
  -> Debug/review/future upgrades can still access FULL INTERNAL MAPS

This is one of the strongest implementation decisions in Floor 3.

SECTION 26 - COMMON MARKET STATE PROJECTION CONTRACT TIGHTENING
================================================================================

This section tightens Section 9 without replacing it.

Common Market State Projection remains a secondary Floor 3 output.
Its role is now made more explicit.

Projection fields (locked):
- price_state
- volatility_state
- session_state
- regime_state
- key_levels

Ownership
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Owner = Floor 3
Builder = common_market_state_projection.py

It is not built by Floor 4.
It is not built by Captain.

Why ownership is important:
This projection is a normalized market-state artifact created from Floor 3
intelligence.  If ownership moves upward, responsibility blur appears.

Refresh Policy
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Projection refresh follows the broader Floor 3 hybrid refresh philosophy.
Meaning:
- it is refreshed when the relevant underlying domain states refresh
- it is not a totally independent free-floating engine
- 1m base refresh can update it
- deeper 5m/15m structural refresh can enrich parts of it through source states

This does not create a new cadence.
It formalizes existing ownership.

Consumers
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Primary conceptual consumers:
- Floor 4 heads (shared context support where relevant)
- floor summary construction support
- dashboard lightweight state consumption
- debugging / state consistency tracing

Captain boundary note:
Captain does not directly consume this projection as a routine runtime path.
Captain consumes Floor 4 outputs.
This projection supports Floor 4 and shared state understanding.

Why this tightening was required:
The audit correctly identified that projection was defined but its ownership and
runtime consumer role were not explicit enough.

SECTION 27 - TIER 1 DEPENDENCY CONTRACT TIGHTENING
================================================================================

This section tightens Section 10 without changing the domain architecture.

Locked dependency flow:

Structure
  ↓
Shared Structure State
  ↓
SMC + ICT Parallel Interpretation

Meaning:
- Structure remains mandatory foundation
- SMC and ICT remain independent domains
- SMC and ICT are strongly linked to structure
- SMC and ICT do not need to be fully serial to each other

Why this is the strongest contract:
- Structure should not be merged into SMC
- SMC should not swallow Structure
- ICT should not be forced into unnecessary serial dependency behind SMC
- but both SMC and ICT need structural truth first

So the contract is:
- Structure publishes foundational structural outputs and shared structure state
- SMC consumes that foundation and interprets smart-money structure context
- ICT consumes that foundation and interprets institutional delivery context
- SMC and ICT remain strongly linked, but parallel after structural foundation

Why this tightening was required:
The audit needed dependency clarity so implementation does not drift into either:
- fully merged confusion
or
- wasteful isolated duplication

SECTION 28 - CANDIDATE GRADE CONTRACT TIGHTENING
================================================================================

This section formalizes candidate_grade as an explicit contract concept.

candidate_grade means:
- candidate quality only

candidate_grade is NOT:
- confidence
- conviction
- trade approval
- final bias

This must remain explicit.

Typical conceptual levels:
- A = candidate quality high
- B = candidate quality medium
- C = candidate quality weak

Why this matters:
If candidate_grade is confused with confidence or conviction,
then Floor 3 starts drifting into Head/Captain territory.

Therefore the architecture law here is:
- candidate_grade helps rank candidate quality
- Floor 4 turns quality into confidence
- Floor 5 turns confidence + context into conviction

This section does not add new calculations.
It only tightens the meaning of an already discussed concept.

SECTION 29 - INTERNAL MAPS vs ACTIVE MAPS CONTRACT TIGHTENING
================================================================================

This section tightens Section 18.

Locked internal map concepts include examples such as:
- full_ob_map
- full_fvg_map
- full_liquidity_map
- full_level_map

Locked active map concepts include examples such as:
- active_ob_map
- active_fvg_map
- active_liquidity_map
- active_level_map

Propagation contract:

Step 1:
Floor 3 domain engines detect and maintain FULL INTERNAL MAPS.
These keep:
- complete domain depth
- debugging support
- historical structural richness
- future extension support

Step 2:
Relevance/activity filtering is applied.
This selects the currently important subset.

Step 3:
ACTIVE MAPS are produced from FULL INTERNAL MAPS.
These are cleaner, lower-noise, and head-consumable.

Step 4:
Floor 4 Heads consume ACTIVE MAPS as their normal map input.

Step 5:
FULL INTERNAL MAPS remain available for:
- debugging
- review
- future extensions
- internal lifecycle management

Why this tightening was required:
The audit correctly found that internal vs active maps idea was good but needed
an explicit propagation rule.

This does not change map architecture.
It clarifies the flow.
