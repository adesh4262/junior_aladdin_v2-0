SECTION 1 - PURPOSE
================================================================================

One-line identity:
SIDE A = Action Layer

Meaning:
Captain decides.
SIDE A executes.

Why SIDE A exists:
A trade decision is not enough.
The system also needs a controlled layer that can:
- route the trade to the correct mode
- perform pre-trade safety checks
- place and manage orders
- maintain position safety
- survive broker uncertainty
- enforce protection rules
- log execution truth
- remain disciplined under live capital risk

This side exists to turn a decision into an operationally safe action.


================================================================================
SECTION 2 - EXECUTION PHILOSOPHY
================================================================================

SIDE A philosophy is governed by one final safety principle:

PROTECT FIRST, THEN OPTIMIZE

Meaning:
1. protection first
2. risk control second
3. execution quality third
4. optimization last

This principle overrides convenience.
It also overrides excessive cleverness.

SIDE A is not trying to be smart.
It is trying to be:
- correct
- safe
- predictable
- recoverable
- consistent

Captain is the Strategic Commander.
Execution is the Tactical Operator.

This means:
- Captain creates the approved intent
- Execution carries it out mechanically
- Captain may intervene only in rare exceptional battlefield conditions


================================================================================
SECTION 3 - STRICT GUARDRAILS
================================================================================

RULE 1
SIDE A does not create trades.
It only receives approved execution intent.

RULE 2
SIDE A does not interpret market meaning.
No SMC/ICT/technical judgment happens here.

RULE 3
SIDE A does not create confidence or conviction.
Those belong to Floors 4 and 5.

RULE 4
SIDE A may reject, delay, shrink, retry, or flatten for safety reasons.
It may never “invent” a better trade.

RULE 5
SIDE A may reduce size for safety.
It may never increase size beyond Captain intent.

RULE 6
After execution start, Captain must not override the entry side.
Captain override is limited to exit/risk-side intervention.

RULE 7
SIDE A must preserve one-trade discipline.
If an active live trade exists, a new execution intent should not become a new
live trade.

RULE 8
Operational uncertainty must be represented honestly.
UNKNOWN / PENDING_RECONCILE states are allowed.

RULE 9
Execution must remain safety-biased under degraded conditions.

RULE 10
Logs must tell the operational truth,
not replace the intelligence layers.

================================================================================
SECTION 6 - MODE ROUTER
================================================================================

Purpose:
Mode Router decides where approved execution intent goes.
It does not judge the trade.
It does not modify market logic.

Responsibilities:
- identify current execution mode
- always trigger alert/info path
- route approved intent to:
  - simulated execution path
  - real broker execution path
- enforce mode transition governance

What Mode Router does NOT do:
- strategy change
- trade approval
- trade re-interpretation
- risk intelligence

Why Mode Router must exist:
If execution core internally mixes ALERT, PAPER, and REAL too deeply,
complexity and testing burden increase.


================================================================================
SECTION 7 - RISK GATE
================================================================================

Locked strength:
Very Strong

Purpose:
The Risk Gate asks only one question:
Can this approved trade be safely and validly executed right now?

It does NOT ask:
Is this a good trade?
That question was already answered above.

Pre-Order Risk Checks:
- available capital
- required capital
- margin availability
- quantity sanity
- lot feasibility
- max loss limits
- mode validation
- real lock state
- duplicate execution prevention
- stale intent detection
- one-trade enforcement
- intent fingerprint duplicate check

Why this gate is strong:
Execution sits closest to capital.
A weak gate here would be dangerous.

Locked size policy:
- Captain size = default truth
- SIDE A never increases size
- reduce-only adjustment allowed if needed for capital/margin/safety reasons
- any reduce-only adjustment must be logged

One-Trade Enforcement Contract
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Locked upstream rule already exists: one active trade at a time.
SIDE A must enforce this operationally as a safety contract.

Enforcement points:
1. at execution intent entry
2. at pre-order risk gate
3. at live state transition if a second active live trade would be created

Behavior:
- if one active live trade already exists, a new execution intent must not become
  a second live trade
- the action should be blocked, not silently ignored
- the block should be recorded in blocked-action journal

Blocked-action guidance:
- severity should be at least CAUTION
- if the second intent appears during already unstable/recovering execution state,
  severity can escalate higher according to current execution conditions

Why this tightening is needed:
Captain follows the one-trade rule by design, but SIDE A must not rely on blind
trust.  SIDE A must act as the final operational safety net against accidental
multi-position creation.

Intent Fingerprint Contract
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SIDE A should maintain a lightweight intent fingerprint contract for duplicate-
intent protection.

Purpose:
- detect accidental duplicate intent submissions
- distinguish same trade_id from materially same execution intent attempts
- improve safe idempotent behavior without changing strategy logic

Minimum contract idea:
- trade_id remains mandatory
- intent_fingerprint is a lightweight execution-safe identity companion
- it is not market intelligence
- it is not Captain conviction
- it is not a new trade decision field

Duplicate-intent handling behavior:
- if trade_id + intent_fingerprint indicates the same execution intent is being
  re-fired unintentionally, SIDE A should block or safely ignore the duplicate
  path according to current lifecycle state
- duplicate-intent blocks should be journaled through blocked-action tracking

Why this tightening is needed:
trade_id alone may not be enough to protect against accidental duplicate
execution attempts under retry, delayed signal routing, or operator/system edge
cases.

Responsibilities:

SECTION 11 - PAPER / REAL HANDLING
================================================================================

PAPER Mode
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Locked target:
Deep realism (as target), but with practical bounded complexity.

Paper should simulate, as realistically as practical:
- slippage
- brokerage / charges
- spread effect
- delay
- rejection possibility
- selective partial fill simulation

Paper capital model:
- default rolling paper account
- optional reset available

REAL Mode
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Locked live philosophy:
- strict live safety
- primary order discipline = LIMIT + SL-LIMIT
- no unnecessary flexible live behavior

Real mode also supports:
- broker communication
- real acknowledgements
- real rejection handling
- real partial fill handling
- real lock / override governance

Paper ≈ Real parity policy:
- high parity on core lifecycle behavior
- selected advanced realism can remain lighter in paper where justified

SECTION 13 - STATE MACHINE
================================================================================

High-level state machine flow:

IDLE
 ↓
INTENT_RECEIVED
 ↓
RISK_PASSED or FAILED
 ↓
ORDER_PENDING
 ↓
PARTIAL_FILL / FILLED / REJECTED / UNKNOWN
 ↓
PROTECTED
 ↓
MANAGING
 ↓
EXITING
 ↓
CLOSED

Exceptional branches:
- LOCKED
- UNKNOWN / PENDING_RECONCILE
- EMERGENCY FLATTEN PATH

State Transition Table
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
| Current State         | Event/Condition                     | Next State                 |
|----------------------|-------------------------------------|----------------------------|
| IDLE                 | Captain intent received             | INTENT_RECEIVED            |
| INTENT_RECEIVED      | Risk gate passed                    | RISK_PASSED                |
| INTENT_RECEIVED      | Risk gate failed                    | FAILED                     |
| RISK_PASSED          | order submitted                     | ORDER_PENDING              |
| ORDER_PENDING        | partial fill                        | PARTIAL_FILL               |
| ORDER_PENDING        | full fill                           | FILLED                     |
| ORDER_PENDING        | reject                              | FAILED                     |
| ORDER_PENDING        | timeout/unclear                     | UNKNOWN / PENDING_RECONCILE|
| PARTIAL_FILL         | protection staged                   | PROTECTED                  |
| FILLED               | protection placed                   | PROTECTED                  |
| PROTECTED            | active management begins            | MANAGING                   |
| MANAGING             | exit condition triggered            | EXITING                    |
| EXITING              | close complete                      | CLOSED                     |
| ANY ACTIVE STATE     | critical lock / emergency flatten   | LOCKED or CLOSED           |
| UNKNOWN              | reconcile success                   | MANAGING / PROTECTED       |
| UNKNOWN              | unrecoverable / fail-safe           | FAILED / CLOSED            |

This table is the behavioral backbone of SIDE A.

Protection must be established as early as practical.

Protect First, Then Optimize

- unprotected live exposure is dangerous

================================================================================
SECTION 15 - PARTIAL FILL HANDLING
================================================================================

Locked policy:
Hybrid

REAL mode:
- partial-fill aware management is mandatory

PAPER mode:
- selective realism simulation is acceptable

Partial fill handling means:
- actual filled quantity tracked
- pending remainder tracked
- SL quantity adjusted
- TGT quantity adjusted
- position truth updated

Why this matters:
Ignoring partial fills can create false risk accounting and false position truth.

Partial fill does NOT mean a new trade.
It means an update to the current trade state.

SECTION 17 - RECOVERY MODEL
================================================================================

Recovery is a first-class execution concern.

Key recovery principles:
- UNKNOWN state must be allowed
- UNKNOWN state must be bounded
- reconcile attempts must exist
- unresolved uncertainty must escalate

Unknown / Pending Reconcile policy:
- short bounded window
- reconcile attempts
- then escalate / flatten / fail-safe

Why:
Unknown state can happen in real markets.
Force-fitting it into failed/rejected immediately may be unsafe.


================================================================================
SECTION 18 - KILL-SWITCH GOVERNANCE
================================================================================

Locked policy:
Hybrid kill-switch behavior

Soft Kill Switch:
- block new entries
- existing trades continue under management

Critical Kill Switch:
- flatten path
- freeze execution

Why this is strongest:
Not all kill events are equal.
A soft safety stop and a full flatten emergency should not be treated the same.

Important note:
Critical flatten path must remain compatible with controlled fallback / safety
flatten philosophy.

Data Health Execution Policy Table
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Execution safety should react to upper-layer operational health facts without
becoming an intelligence or decision layer.

Suggested contract-level behavior mapping:

| Data Health State | Execution Behavior |
|------------------|--------------------|
| DEGRADED         | allow execution with stricter caution/risk checks |
| STALE            | block new entries; existing protected trades continue under management |
| CRITICAL         | trigger safety escalation / lock path / flatten policy as already governed |

Important rule:
- these states do NOT become trade intelligence
- these states do NOT generate directional bias
- these states do NOT replace Captain's final authority
- they are operational safety facts that influence execution caution behavior

Why this tightening is needed:
Execution already had degraded/stale safety philosophy locked through Q33, but
this table makes the contract explicit and implementation-readable.
